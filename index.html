About

I am a MSc Bioinformatics student at the University of Edinburgh, coming from a joint BSc in Nanobiology at TU Delft and Erasmus University Rotterdam, where I graduated with a 7.5/10. That degree was genuinely interdisciplinary: biology, physics, chemistry, maths, and computation. That mix has shaped how I approach problems; I tend to move between abstraction levels until something clicks.

My MSc industry placement is at CEXAL Ltd, a diagnostics biotech, where I'm building automated bioinformatics pipelines for nucleic acid detection probe design. Sequence parsing, secondary structure prediction, BLAST specificity screening, ranked synthesis-ready outputs. The kind of work where the biology and the software engineering are equally load-bearing.

Before that I was doing imaging QC and 3D bioprinting experiments at TU Delft's Shaping Matter Lab, and before that teaching physics and philosophy to 120+ undergraduates. I like problems that sit at the intersection of disciplines, where a borrowed technique from one field becomes genuinely useful in another.

I'm interested in diagnostics, scalable molecular detection, data pipelines, and biomanufacturing. Available for roles from August/September 2026. UK Graduate Visa eligible on graduation, so no sponsorship required for UK roles.

PRIMED TRY Prediction Pipeline

1st Place · Hackiest Hack · PRIMED Edinburgh Biohackathon 2026

Built in 43 hours. Genome-scale metabolic modelling tool for fungal strain TRY (Titer, Rate, Yield) prediction. Strain detection via NCBI Taxonomy, SBML model generation, in-browser Flux Balance Analysis, Michaelis-Menten kinetics, Snakemake-style orchestration.

Python JavaScript PHP SQL SBML Flux Balance Analysis NCBI Taxonomy

Lab Grown Wood

BSc Thesis · TU Delft · Thesis grade: 8.5/10

Investigated the feasibility of lab-grown wood through 3D printing of plant cells, evaluating growth, viability, and structural fidelity over time. Combined biological experimentation with additive manufacturing to assess how printing parameters and growth conditions affect cell survival, proliferation, and shape retention. Manuscript in preparation.

3D Bioprinting Plant Cell Culture Materials Science Biofabrication ImageJ

Bio-Inspired Breast Pump Flange Insert

BME Minor Design Project · TU Delft · 2025 · in consultation with Reinier de Graaf Hospital

Designed and prototyped a biologically accurate breast pump flange insert in a team of five. Developed a tongue-inspired compression mechanism applying positive pressure to mimic infant suckling, bellows actuators for peripheral breast compression, a ribbed surface for massage, and an adjustable geometry accommodating a wider nipple size range. Prototyped in SolidWorks using TPU and biomedical-grade elastic resin.

SolidWorks CAD Design Prototyping Biomedical Engineering R&D

Advanced Maths Simulations
